    ## 1. Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design? ­
       
       Draw It or Lose It, is a game that was developed by the company The Gaming Room, which was originally available solely on Android devices. The client wanted to expand the application into a web-based platform in which multiple operating systems could be supported, including desktop and mobile browsers. Multiple players and teams needed to be supported by the software, to enforce unique names for all entities, and to maintain a consistent real-time game state. The aim of this was to create an accessible and scalable multiplayer experience and simultaneously preserve the core gameplay. 

    ## 2. What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation? 
	
	Clearly organizing the system architecture and domain model was one of the various aspects 	that I did particularly well. Object-oriented principles such as inheritance, encapsulation, and 	design patterns like the singleton were used within the system. The relationships between 	Game, Team, Player, and GameService were clearly defined, making the structure of the 	application easy to understand. In addition, the overall quality of the product was strengthened 	due to the inclusion of detailed evaluations of different operating platforms.

    ## 3. What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code? 

	Working through the design document helped me better understand the overall structure and 	requirements of the system before writing any code. It allowed me to focus and think through 	the important decisions like system architecture, platform compatibility, and data management. 	Many potential issues were already considered and addressed, and for that reason, the 	development was made more efficient. The design document helped reduce confusion and 	maintain consistency throughout development.

    ## 4. If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it? 

	If there could be one area for revision in the document, I would improve the Evaluation section; 	It would ideally be more focused and concise. While it provides detailed comparisons of 	operating systems and environments, some areas could be streamlined to improve readability. It 	is critical to be clear in a document such as this one, one reason would be to remove 	redundancies and make the wording more refined. These changes would make the key points 	made stand out more effectively for the reader.
       
    ## 5. How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing? 

	I interpreted the user’s needs by focusing on accessibility, performance, and ease of use. Unique 	identifiers and consistent game state management were implemented in order to improve the 	user experience. When considering the needs of the users, it is important because it ensures that 	the final product works properly, is intuitive, and engaging. The software may be prone to 	failure, particularly not meeting the user’s expectations or even be difficult to use if the users 	needs are not prioritized.
 
    ## 6. How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application? 
	
	I approached designing the software by first analyzing the requirements and then breaking the 	system into smaller, manageable components. Object-oriented design principles and design 	patterns such as the singleton were used to structure the application effectively. I also 	considered system architecture and platform compatibility early in the process. Strategies like 	these will continue to be use by me in the future, along with creating more detailed diagrams 	and incorporating user feedback earlier in the design phase in order to further improve the 	overall quality of the application.
